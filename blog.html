My friend since 7th grade is coincidentally in Paris for two weeks doing a school summer program. So we couldn't forget to do our 7 year old tradition...

Not only was this weekend to celebrate France, but also my 2 friends I met in the program! They both turned 21, so of course, as any American does, we celebrated. July 5, 2025 Travel

42.0 hours in Amsterdam

Chat. It was epic. My solo adventure became a 3 person extravaganza, and it was the best time of my life.

I casually mentioned to some friends in the program that I was going to Amsterdam for travel and lodging all under $200, and immediately 2 people wanted to join me. We were only there for one night, but it was a perfect amount. My friend Payton and I went on an earlier train and shopped around the city for a bit. We walked and talked and ate some amazing food. Of course there were coffeeshops around there but we didn't visit them (..yet) I saw the most wonderful vintage designer bag and it took everything in me not to get it.

I hadn't gotten much time to talk to Payton beforehand but she is literally the nicest person ever. I always get a bit of social anxiety when talking to people one-on-one but she was so genuine and cool. Later we went on a canal tour because one must in Amsterdam. After that she found a lovely waterfront dinner that we lowkey had trouble finding but ended up being delicious in the end. We then made our way to Utrecht because I initially booked my hostel there since I wanted to visit the university. omg and there was so much Miffy there. It's where the silly little bunny was originated and they even had a Miffy spotlight. We got drinks and the hostel was very nice while we waited for our other friend Evelyn to arrive. The minute she arrived we went full-on Amsterdam hehehe.

Sleep is for the weak, so we got up at 7:30 am and made our way back to Amsterdam to make it for our lil bike tour of the city (typical Amsterdam fashion). We barely made it in time for the tour and we were the only Americans but the tour guide was nice and funny. After, our guide recommended us to go to the World's Largest Flea Market, with over 750 stalls. It was heaven and I got so so many knick knacks. They even had flip phones, but not the ferrari one I want :/ We made it back for our train and arrived right back home where I immediately turned around and went out lol to visit my friend Felicity who is here for a few weeks.

July 3, 2025 Food

Baby Bottle Wine Fondue Place

The title speaks for itself...

The title speaks for itself. Not much to share about it other than it was amazing. It is this little hole in the wall in Montmartre that only serves fondue and wine from baby bottles. Some people even had to climb over the table to sit in their seat. It was filled with writing from customers on the walls, chairs, tables, you name it. Of course we put a little mark on the wall :D We also struck up a really nice and fun conversation with some parents who were sitting next to us while their kids were at a different table. Safe to say we WILL be back.

June 28, 2025 Culture

Baby's First Fête de la Musique and Pride

There's a first time for everything!

There's a first time for everything! While not in Paris, Bordeaux had a fun Fête de la Musique. It was centered mostly in downtown Bordeaux so we could definitely leave if we got overstimulated. That said, the alleyways were small so it seemed a lot more packed than I think it was. Me and my roommate from the program Evelyn went out on the town and we had a blast. We caught our eyes on the first outdoor beer booth and immediately chugged down a pint. It made me queasy for a bit but my stomach is always upset (more on this later). Anyways, we were dancing around town, so two marching bands, and sooo many high schoolers off their butts. After a bit of crowdwork (navigating through gridlock) we sat down an watched with everyone at the restaurant the Bordeaux v. Marseille rugby match. Holy cow that was brutal. I don't fully understand the culture of knocking people down for balls but oh well... it was a fun environment. Then we engaged in some deep talk and gossip over drinkies and I witnessed Evelyn absolutely nailing a cartwheel on the street. Overall 10/10 would do again

Btw. turns out my "upset stomach" was actually food poisoning from the salmon we had at the group dinner, as 3 other girls fells absolutely ill as well.

My next first was finally going to pride (yippee) I haven't gone before because no one really wanted to go with me :/ since my hometown friends don't like to go out, so it was a fun refresher. I went with a group of girls from the program and I had been looking forward to it since Paris Pride is supposed to be lit. AND. IT. WAS. I got to see drag queens on 4x4 trucks blasting house and Lady Gaga. We walked the last leg with the parade and made it to the end before crashing out back at the hotel. It was so so hot and muggy though and I got mosquito bites ALL over my leg--they are seriously homophobic. It felt so freeing being there and it will definitely not be my last!

June 23, 2025 Travel

Lost in Bordeaux

My first trip away from Paris fr fr. I went with the internship program and it was lit af. Wine country of course so I got to indulge, legally, in some classics of the area...

My first trip away from Paris fr fr. I went with the internship program and it was lit af. Wine country of course so I got to indulge, legally, in some classics of the area. At first, we got there at like 11pm at night and I was tired af but also very very hungry. Turns out one of the girls that I didn't know so well but went out once with was my roommate so we got gyros at a random open late place because even McDonalds was closed. Turns out we were not in the safest part of Bordeaux and we had to walk through some sketchy places--luckily Berkeley has prepared me lol. The next day we had a walking tour to attend and before that me and my roommate and some other girls visited the cathedral in Downtown and went shopping through some streets. It was the summer solstice aka Fête de la Musique day, so all the shopowners were preparing for it by setting up DJ booths and tables outside.

We went on a walking tour while it was so so hot though and now I feel burnt to oblivion. We saw and learned all the major history of the city, and it was supposed to end with wine and cheese, but since the internship is a dry program (for their activities), we had a scrumptious plate of cheese.

The other night was of course Fête de la Musique, which was pretty fun but it deserves its own blog post. The next day, I went solo questing around because instead of the included lunch, I wanted to explore the city. I visited a nice museum and gardens and shopped for a bit.

The craziest part of the trip was actually getting back--believe it or not. Our train got cancelled due to mechanical issues and there was no AC or wifi and my phone was about to die. I was also all alone in my train car lol. Since we were all separated, half of the group was told that they should board another train inbound to Paris, but the other half (including myself and the group leaders) were not told this because we were in the last cars. So, we literally saw half the group leave without us. AND THIS WAS IN A HEAT WAVE!! So we had to sit around the hot, muggy train station for another 2 hours and then they emergency cleaned and made us board another train. When we finally got back it was like 11pm, and I still had Cal homework to do for the summer class, but I was hungry af and I thought I would treat myself with UberEats...TURNS OUT I ORDERED FOR PICK UP. So imagine me walking 10 minutes at midnight, tired and starving without my homework being started (it was due in a few hours) all to get a mid burger. Ultimately, your girl ate and locked in and completed all her stuff and finally got a few hours to sleep before my 7:30am wake up for my internship.

but at least i survived.
